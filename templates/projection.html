Projecting the impact of 2016 legislation

We decided to project what the sales would be like for different states voting on marijuana legislation in 2016. One thing we noticed was that sales for the first two years in Washington seemed to steadily increase each month and by a constant amount, so we simply decided to project a revenue growth per month. In order to come up with a projection, we first collected the percentage of people 18-25 and 25+ who had smoked marijuana within the past month by state from a Washington Post article. We then collected the state populations by age from the Kaiser Family Foundation . From these numbers, we were able to roughly calculate the total number of marijuana users by state. Next, we calculated an average dollar per marijuana smoker in Washington and Colorado and averaged these numbers together. This average was then multiplied by the number of marijuana smokers in each state to get a projected monthly revenue increase for each state.
